Spaced Repetition Settings

How many brand-new cards to introduce each day.

A daily cap so due reviews never pile up into an overwhelming session.

Higher difficulty = shorter gaps = more reviews. Lower = longer gaps = fewer reviews.

The idea. Instead of studying every card every time, each card comes back right before you are likely to forget it. Cards you know well are shown less and less often; cards you struggle with come back sooner. This is the most efficient way to move things into long-term memory.

New cards per day. Your daily intake of fresh material. Learning a card also creates future reviews, so a bigger number means more reviews building up over time. If you ever feel overwhelmed, lower this for a while and focus on your reviews, then raise it again later.

Maximum reviews per day. A safety cap. On large decks, failed cards can stack up into hundreds of due reviews. This limit keeps each day manageable — any extra due cards simply wait for the next day.

Difficulty. Controls how fast the gaps between reviews grow. High grows gaps slowly, so you review more often (safer, more work). Low grows gaps quickly, so you review less often (faster, riskier). Normal is a balanced default.

A day ends at midnight. "Tomorrow" means the next calendar day at 00:00 on your device — not 24 hours after you finish. Your daily New and Review counts also reset at midnight.

Grading this deck (flip cards). After flipping, you rate how it went:

Again — you forgot. The card comes back within a few minutes this same session; if it was already learned, its interval shrinks and its ease drops a little.

Good — you recalled it. A new card moves toward graduating; a learned card's interval grows by its ease factor.

Easy — it was effortless. The card jumps to a noticeably longer interval than Good.

Your Difficulty setting then scales all of these gaps: High = shorter gaps (more reviews), Low = longer gaps (fewer reviews).

Grading this deck (multiple choice). There are no self-rating buttons — your answer is graded for you:

Correct answer counts as Good: the card's interval grows and it comes back further in the future.

Wrong answer counts as Again: the card resets and comes back within a few minutes this same session, and (if it was already learned) its interval shrinks.

Your Difficulty setting then scales the gaps between reviews: High = shorter gaps (more reviews), Low = longer gaps (fewer reviews).

Frequently asked questions

What if I miss a day?
Nothing bad happens. The reviews that were due simply wait for you — no penalty, no lost progress. Next time you study they'll be there (up to your daily review cap).

Why do I only see a few cards when the deck is huge?
That's the whole point. You learn a few new cards a day and each one returns only when it's due. This spreads the work out and beats cramming.

A new card keeps reappearing in the same session — is that broken?
No. A brand-new card must be answered correctly a couple of times, a few minutes apart, before it "graduates" to a day-level schedule. That short repetition helps it stick.

Can I keep studying after "Done for today"?
Yes — use Extra study. It gives you a batch of random cards you've already seen, purely for practice. It never changes your schedule or tomorrow's reviews.

What do the top numbers mean?
New / Learning / To Review are what's waiting for you today; Total is how many cards the deck has in all.

Does my progress sync between phone and computer?
No. It's saved locally in each browser, so your phone and your computer track progress independently.

Will I ever fully finish a deck?
Not "forever" — that's how long-term memory works. But once cards are scheduled far into the future, your daily load becomes very small.

You can change these settings any time. Changes apply going forward and never erase your progress.
